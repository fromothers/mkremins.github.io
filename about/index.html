About me, about this site

I'm Max Kreminski. I design interactions, program computers, write words, and study Interactive Media & Games as an undergraduate student at USC.

My current favorite programming languages are Clojure and Elm. Significant ongoing open-source projects of mine include:

  • Flense – an experimental structural editor for Clojure code
  • Fanciful – an expressive text formatting library for Bukkit plugins

Exquisite Corpus is my blog. Expect (infrequent) posts about: UX/UI/HCI; explorable explanations; ways to make computer programming more human-friendly; software from the utopian future; cogsci & education; compsci & functional programming; game design & procedural rhetoric; and whatever else happens to be on my mind.

You should follow me on Twitter. If you want, you can also email me; my address is just my name, as given above, at Gmail.
